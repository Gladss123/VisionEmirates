Bedouin Life üèïÔ∏è

Nomadic tribes roamed the vast desert, living in harmony with the harsh yet beautiful landscape. The history of the UAE is diverse and immersive, brimming with tales of nomadic Arabs, or Bedouins, who once inhabited the region. Known for their unique lifestyle and hardy resourcefulness, they are an intrinsic part of the region and its heritage. Here are some of the most fascinating facts about Bedouin life in the UAE. Archaeological evidence shows that Bedouin herders lived in the UAE, dwelling in a mangrove swamp area in Dubai between 2500 BCE and 3000 BCE. Back then, the community was into date palm cultivation. Famous for their honour, hospitality and courage, Bedouins survived water scarcity and other resources to create a life full of simple but beautiful traditions. For centuries, Bedouins moved from one place to another in the region in nomadic cycles to find water, fertile land and suitable weather conditions.
