1820

General Maritime Treaty signed with Britain ‚Äî securing peace for the trading coast.In 1820, the General Maritime Treaty was signed between the British government and the rulers of the coastal tribes of the southeastern Arabian Peninsula, including areas of present-day UAE. 1971

On December 2, 1971, the United Arab Emirates (UAE) was officially formed as a union of seven emirates: Abu Dhabi, Dubai, Sharjah, Ajman, Umm Al Quwain, and Fujairah. Ras Al Khaimah joined shortly after in February 1972. Led by Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan, the founding father and first President, this unification marked a historic moment of cooperation, laying the foundation for a strong, prosperous, and modern nation.

1980s

Following the formation of the UAE, the country experienced a massive infrastructure boom, especially from the 1980s onward. Fueled by oil revenues and visionary leadership, the UAE rapidly developed modern cities, world-class airports, ports, highways, and public services. Iconic landmarks like Burj Khalifa, Dubai International Airport, and Jebel Ali Port symbolized its rise as a global hub for business, tourism, and trade, connecting the East and West like never before.

2000s

UAE becomes a global hub for business, culture, and innovation. In recent decades, the UAE has emerged as a global hub for business, culture, and innovation. With cities like Dubai and Abu Dhabi leading the way, the country has attracted international companies, hosted world expos, launched space missions, and built cutting-edge cultural landmarks like the Louvre Abu Dhabi. Strategic investments in technology, renewable energy, education, and infrastructure have positioned the UAE as a leader in the Middle East and a key player on the world stage.
